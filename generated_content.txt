Title: DeepL launches DeepL Voice, real-time, text-based translations from voices and videos

 Language AI startup DeepL has launched DeepL Voice, a new feature for its translation tool that provides real-time translations of voices and videos. 

Previously, DeepL's tool focused on translating text and handwritten language, but DeepL Voice extends its capabilities to translate spoken language both in real-time and from videos. 

The company has achieved this by deploying artificial intelligence techniques centred on multi-modal translation, according to DeepL's blog post announcing the feature. 

Multi-modal translation is when AI models translate various forms of communication, be it voice, text, or image-based, in tandem with one another to provide a holistic translation. 

For example, with DeepL Voice, you could use the tool to translate a video of someone speaking in another language, either by typing out the spoken words in real-time or by pasting a DeepL-generated transcript of the video beforehand. 

Alternatively, the new feature can translate on-demand, meaning you can grab clips from videos or audio that weren't pre-transcribed and have them translated instantly. 

This is made possible by a combination of speech recognition — which guarantees the transcript is correct — and translation, all in a fraction of a second. 

DeepL Voice was trained on hundreds of hours of multilingual spoken data from various sources, including the internet, to produce accurate translations.
